## Understanding Yoga Blocks

### What Are Yoga Blocks?

Yoga blocks are versatile props designed for stability and support during yoga practice. They help you achieve proper alignment, allowing you to stretch deeper and hold poses comfortably. I’ve personally found that using blocks has improved my practice by enhancing my flexibility and enabling me to explore more challenging poses without strain.

### Who Should Use Yoga Blocks?

- **Beginners**: Many newcomers find that blocks can help establish form and stability, making poses more accessible.
- **Advanced Yogis**: Even seasoned practitioners can benefit from blocks for deepening stretches or modifying challenging poses.
- **Individuals with Injuries**: Blocks can offer support for those recovering from injuries by providing a stable base.

## The Importance of Material Choice

### Why Your Block’s Material Matters

The material of your yoga block significantly affects your practice. Each type offers distinct benefits:

- **Cork**: Excellent grip and sturdiness, ideal for advanced poses.
- **Foam**: Lightweight and soft, but may not provide as much support.

Through my testing, I found that cork blocks like the Liforme had a noticeable grip which helped during challenging poses, while foam blocks like AmazonBasics were easier to handle and transport, especially when I practiced outdoors or traveled.

### Durability vs. Weight

For instance, while cork blocks are heavier, they lend stability in balance poses. Conversely, foam blocks are easier to move but can compress over time if not carefully handled. I once accidentally dropped a foam block, and it sustained a dent that affected its performance.
