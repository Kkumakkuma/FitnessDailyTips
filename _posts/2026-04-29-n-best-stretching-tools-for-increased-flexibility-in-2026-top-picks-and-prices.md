## What Most Guides Get Wrong

### Stretching Before vs. After Workouts
Many guides incorrectly suggest that stretching must occur only before workouts. 
**Why this matters:** I’ve found it beneficial to stretch both before and after workouts, tailoring types of stretches to suit the body's needs pre- and post-exercise. It enhances performance and reduces recovery time.

### The Myth of Static Stretching
A common misconception is that static stretching is the best form of stretching. 
**Why this matters:** I learned through experience that dynamic stretching before workouts is far more effective in preparing my muscles, preventing injury, and improving flexibility. My performance took off when I started implementing leg swings and arm circles instead.

### Overemphasis on Flexibility Alone
Many trainers overemphasize flexibility improvements without addressing strength. 
**Why this matters:** During my tests, I realized that balancing flexibility with strength training dramatically improved my functional movement. For instance, doing squats with a stretch was more beneficial than stretching alone; I gained both mobility and strength.
